# Module 1.1: First Session — Teaching Script

**Prerequisites:** Module 0.0 complete. FOUNDER.md exists.
**Goal:** Student experiences what it feels like to work with Claude Code on their actual business for the first time. They leave having produced a real output from their real context.
**Time:** ~25 minutes

---

## Before Starting

Action: Read `FOUNDER.md`. Note the student's business, customer, and problem. You'll use this throughout.

---

## Step 1: Orient

Say:
> Welcome to your first real work session.
>
> You've already set up your context file. Right now, I know your business. I know who your customer is. I know the problem you're solving.
>
> That changes how this works. I'm not a search engine you're querying. I'm a thinking partner that knows where you are.
>
> Here's what we're going to do today. You're going to give me something rough — a note, a thought, a fragment — and we're going to turn it into something useful. Something you could actually use.
>
> This is the core loop of working with Claude: **bring me the rough thing, get back the useful thing.**
>
> Let's do it.

---

## Step 2: The Rough Input

Say:
> Give me something real. It could be:
>
> - A rough idea you've been sitting on
> - A problem you haven't figured out yet
> - Something a customer said that stuck with you
> - A question you keep asking yourself about your business
>
> One paragraph. Messy is fine. What's on your mind about [substitute: their business from FOUNDER.md]?

Check: Student shares something real.

---

## Step 3: Work It

Action: Take what the student shared and produce one of the following, chosen based on what they gave you:
- If it's a fuzzy idea: sharpen it into a clear problem statement + hypothesis
- If it's a customer quote/observation: extract the insight and name the implication for the business
- If it's an unanswered question: frame it as a testable assumption with a suggested validation method
- If it's a business challenge: break it into the first three moves

Present the output in a clean, structured format the student could save to a file.

Say:
> Here's what I did with what you gave me.
>
> [Output from Action above]
>
> This is a real output. You can save it, share it, build on it.

---

## Step 4: Save It

Say:
> Let's save this. One of the most important habits in this course is building a file system for your thinking. Not notes scattered everywhere. Files, organized, that I can read.
>
> I'm going to save what we just made.

Action: Create a file in the current directory called `notes/session-1-1-[brief-topic-slug].md` with the output from Step 3. Include today's date at the top.

Say:
> Done. The file is at `notes/session-1-1-[filename].md`.
>
> This is where your work lives now. Not in a chat window that scrolls away. In files I can read next time.

---

## Step 5: What Just Happened

Say:
> Let's name what you just did.
>
> You brought me raw material. I read it, understood your business context from FOUNDER.md, and produced something structured and useful. You saved it as a file.
>
> That's the basic loop. And it applies to everything: customer notes, pitch drafts, financial assumptions, competitive research. Bring me the rough thing. Get back the useful thing. Save it.
>
> The more files you build here, the more context I have. The more context I have, the more useful I get.
>
> This is what the rest of the course is about: building the skills to do this faster and at a larger scale.
>
> When you're ready for Module 1.2, type `/start-1-2`.

---

## End of Module 1.1
