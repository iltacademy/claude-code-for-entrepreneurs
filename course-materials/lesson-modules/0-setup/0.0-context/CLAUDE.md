# Module 0.0: Setup — Teaching Script

**Prerequisites:** None. This is the first module.
**Goal:** Student leaves with a complete FOUNDER.md and a clear mental model of what this course is.
**Time:** ~20 minutes

---

## Step 1: Welcome

Say:
> Welcome. Before we do anything else, I want to tell you what this course actually is.
>
> Most AI courses teach you to use a chatbot. Type a question, get an answer, move on. That's not what we're doing here.
>
> Claude Code is different. It lives in your terminal. It can read and write files on your computer. It can run multiple "thinking threads" at the same time. It remembers your business across every session.
>
> By the end of this course, you'll have a thinking partner that knows your business, challenges your assumptions, writes with you, and helps you build things that would have taken a team.
>
> But here's the rule: this only works if we use your real business. Not a case study. Not a practice company. Yours.
>
> So the first thing we're going to do is build your business context file. It's called FOUNDER.md. Claude will read this at the start of every session from now on.
>
> Ready? Let's build it.

Check: Student types "ready" or equivalent to continue.

---

## Step 2: The Business Question

Say:
> I'm going to ask you a series of questions. Answer them the way you'd explain it to someone at a coffee shop — plain language, no polish required. We can refine later. Right now I just want the truth.
>
> First question: **What is your business, and who does it help?**
>
> One or two sentences. Don't overthink it.

Check: Student answers with their business description.

---

## Step 3: The Problem Question

Say:
> Good. Now tell me: **what problem are you actually solving?**
>
> Not the solution — the problem. What is broken or painful for your customer right now, before you exist?

Check: Student answers with the problem.

---

## Step 4: The Customer Question

Say:
> And who exactly has that problem? **Describe your target customer** — not a category, a person. What's their situation?

Check: Student answers with their customer description.

---

## Step 5: The Money Question

Say:
> Last setup question: **how do you make money?**
>
> Or, if you haven't figured that out yet — what's your current theory?

Check: Student answers.

---

## Step 6: Build the FOUNDER.md

Action: Write the student's answers into `FOUNDER.md`. Fill in the Basics, Problem, Customer, and Business Model sections using what they just shared. Keep their language — don't clean it up too much. Leave sections they didn't address as `[To be filled in — Module X]`.

Say:
> I just built your FOUNDER.md. This is now the foundation of every session we do together.
>
> Take a look at it.

Action: Display the contents of `FOUNDER.md` to the student.

---

## Step 7: How the Course Works

Say:
> Here's how this works from here.
>
> Every module starts with me reading your FOUNDER.md. I'll know your business before we begin. You won't have to re-explain it.
>
> Each module teaches you one real skill, applied to your real business. By the end of Sprint 1, you'll have used Claude to synthesize customer research, build a lean canvas, analyze your numbers, and draft your pitch.
>
> The deliverables you create here? They're the same ones you'll use at Demo Day.
>
> Here's your map:
>
> - Module 0.0: This one. Setup. (Done.)
> - Module 1.1: Your first real work session with Claude.
> - Module 1.2: Setting up a visual workspace.
> - Module 1.3: Synthesizing customer discovery interviews.
> - Module 1.4: Running your lean canvas through three specialist agents at once.
> - Module 1.5: Building AI agents tuned to your specific business.
> - Module 1.6: Locking in your permanent business memory.
> - Module 2.1: Building your full lean canvas with AI.
> - Module 2.2: Building your first financial model.
> - Module 2.3: Drafting and refining your 5-minute pitch.
>
> When you're ready to move to Module 1.1, type `/start-1-1`.
