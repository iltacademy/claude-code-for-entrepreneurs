What Is This Course?

This course is built inside Claude Code — the AI tool you use is the classroom. There's no video to watch, no slides to flip through. You open the repo, type a command, and Claude teaches the lesson interactively using your real business as the material.

By the end, you'll have five real deliverables — the same ones ILT Academy requires for Founder Showcase and Demo Day.

The core difference from every other AI course: you don't work on a fake example company. In Module 0.0, you build a file called FOUNDER.md from your actual answers. Every module after that reads your file first — so every exercise, every agent conversation, and every deliverable is about your business.

The lean canvas is your canvas. The proforma uses your numbers. The pitch is your pitch.

Meet Your AI Panel

Starting in Module 1.4, you'll work with three built-in AI agents — each with a distinct perspective, a distinct voice, and a job to do. They don't agree with each other. That tension is the point.

You activate them with a simple @name mention inside Claude Code, or by prompting Claude to bring them into the conversation. In Module 1.4, all three are called simultaneously — and the experience is a genuine "wow" moment.

💰
THE INVESTOR
Stress-tests your business model
Asks the questions a real investor asks before the meeting. What's the market size? Why you? Why now? Where does the model break? What would it take to change my mind? The Investor isn't trying to fund you — it's trying to find the holes before they become problems on stage.
@investor — activate in any Claude Code session
🧑
THE CUSTOMER
Speaks as your end user
Reads FOUNDER.md and then talks back as your actual target customer — in first person, with real reactions. Would they try it? Would they pay for it? What are they already using instead? The Customer doesn't care about your vision. It cares whether your solution changes its day.
@customer — activate in any Claude Code session
⚔️
THE COMPETITOR
Maps your competitive landscape
Maps who's already in your space, who's close enough to be a threat, and where the real gaps are. Asks whether your moat is real or just claimed. Points to what incumbents do well and what they leave exposed. Makes you explain your defensibility before you're asked on a stage.
@competitor — activate in any Claude Code session
The Panel Review (Module 1.4 + 2.1)

In Modules 1.4 and 2.1, you'll trigger all three agents at the same time — submitting your lean canvas for a full panel review. Each agent responds from its perspective. You read three different takes on the same document. Then you revise.

This is the closest simulation of a real pitch panel you can run in 25 minutes. Most founders say this is the moment the course earns it.
